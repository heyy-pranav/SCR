About the Program

JIT Green Sunday is a student-led environmental initiative that brings together communities through tree adoption and plantation activities. Our program focuses on creating lasting environmental impact while fostering social connections and personal responsibility towards nature.

Through regular plantation drives, educational workshops, and community engagement, we aim to create a network of environmental stewards committed to preserving and enhancing our natural heritage.

My Tree Adoption

Objectives

  • Promote environmental awareness and responsibility among students and community members
  • Create a personal connection between individuals and nature through tree adoption
  • Contribute to local biodiversity and ecosystem health
  • Foster long-term commitment to environmental stewardship
  • Build a network of tree guardians who monitor and care for adopted trees

Folklore & Usage

Okra (Bhindi)

Scientific Name: Abelmoschus esculentus

Culinary Uses: Okra is a versatile vegetable used in various cuisines. It's commonly prepared as a curry, fried, or added to soups and stews. The mucilaginous texture makes it ideal for thickening dishes.

Ayurvedic Properties: In Ayurveda, okra is considered cooling and is used to balance Pitta dosha. It's believed to support digestive health, help manage blood sugar levels, and provide essential vitamins and minerals.

Folklore: In many cultures, okra is associated with prosperity and abundance. Some traditions hold that planting okra near the home brings good fortune. The plant's ability to produce continuously throughout the season symbolizes persistence and resilience.

Chilli (Mirchi)

Scientific Name: Capsicum annuum

Culinary Uses: Chilli peppers are essential in many cuisines, adding heat and flavor to dishes. They can be used fresh, dried, or powdered, and are integral to spice blends and condiments.

Ayurvedic Properties: Chilli is considered heating in Ayurveda and is used to balance Kapha dosha. It's believed to stimulate digestion, improve circulation, and boost metabolism. However, moderation is key, as excessive consumption can aggravate Pitta.

Folklore: Chilli peppers have been used in various cultures for protection and purification. In some traditions, they are believed to ward off negative energy. The vibrant red color is often associated with vitality and passion. In agricultural folklore, chillies are sometimes planted as companion plants to protect other crops from pests.
